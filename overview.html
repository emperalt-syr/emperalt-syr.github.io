Project Overview

What is FirstPath?

A mobile app built for first-generation immigrant students who are navigating college without a roadmap.

Context

The problem we set out to solve

First-generation immigrant students — students whose parents were born outside the U.S. and who are often the first in their family to navigate the American education system alone — make up a massive and growing share of higher education. Yet they consistently face barriers in finding and accessing the resources available to them. The problem isn't a lack of resources. Scholarships, tutoring programs, career services, and financial aid all exist. The problem is that they're invisible to the students who need them most, and access is largely determined by luck and who you happen to know.

Target users

The primary users are first-generation immigrant students — and more broadly, any first-gen student who doesn't have a built-in support system for navigating higher education. These students are resourceful people navigating a system that wasn't designed for them. The gap is in the system, not in them.

Secondary stakeholders

Academic advisors, financial aid offices, campus cultural centers, and peer mentors are secondary stakeholders — they currently hold a lot of this information but don't always have the resources to reach every student who needs it.

Key features

FirstPath addresses three core tasks: finding and saving a relevant resource through a personalized feed, using an eligibility checker to avoid wasted effort before applying, and setting deadline alerts that surface opportunities before students even think to look for them.

Design principles

Plain language over jargon. No required account creation. Privacy-first data collection. Peer voices built into every listing. A design that signals these resources were made for first-gen students — not just adapted for them as an afterthought.

User Persona

Meet Sofia

Sofia is the most important user for FirstPath. Every design decision was evaluated against her experience.

SM
Sofia Mendez
Age 20 · Sophomore · Communication and Rhetorical Studies · Syracuse University

Background

Dominican immigrant family. First in her family to go to college. Works part-time, sends money home, navigates everything on her own.

Goals

Find scholarships she actually qualifies for without spending hours on confusing websites that weren't designed for her.

Frustrations

Portals feel overwhelming. Eligibility criteria are unreadable. Uncomfortable asking advisors for help. Worried about sharing personal info.

Technology use

Primarily her phone. Short sessions between classes. No patience for slow or confusing apps that require a lot of setup.

"I know the resources are out there. I just always seem to find out about them after it's too late."
