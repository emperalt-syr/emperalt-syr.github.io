Heuristic Evaluation

What another team found

Using Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics to identify issues in the lo-fi prototype and drive improvements in the final hi-fi design.

Evaluation Details

How the evaluation was conducted

The heuristic evaluation was conducted by a peer team reviewing the FirstPath prototype against Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics. Each issue was rated on a severity scale from 0 (no problem) to 4 (usability catastrophe). The findings were then used to drive specific revisions in the final high-fidelity prototype.

Findings

Issues identified and fixes applied

Heuristic Issue Found in Lo-Fi Prototype Severity Fix in Hi-Fi Prototype
Visibility of System Status After saving a scholarship the reminder date was cut off on the confirmation screen — users couldn't tell exactly when they would be notified Severity 2 Updated the confirmation screen to display the full reminder date clearly with a bell icon, a lock screen notification preview, and a full summary of what was saved
Error Prevention The resource feed only showed a "You likely qualify" badge on one listing — users had to open every card just to find out if it was worth their time Severity 3 Added an eligibility status indicator to every card on the feed so users can decide what's worth tapping before committing any time
Help and Documentation The profile setup screen asked for personal background information with no explanation of why it was needed or how it would be used Severity 2 Added a short privacy note — "We never share your info. Your answers only help us match you with the right resources" — to build trust before users fill anything out
Consistency and Standards There was no language selector anywhere in the lo-fi prototype — a non-English speaker would have no way to switch languages before being asked to fill out a form Severity 3 Language selector added to the onboarding flow so users can switch to Spanish or another supported language before entering any personal information
Recognition Rather Than Recall The eligibility checker was not visible on the resource detail screen — users had to already know it existed to look for it Severity 3 Made "Check my eligibility" a large, prominent link directly on the resource feed card and detail screen, with a note that it takes under one minute
What Changed

Lo-fi to hi-fi: key transitions

What the Lo-Fi Prototype Had What the Hi-Fi Prototype Changed Why
No language selector anywhere in the flow Language selector added as the first screen users see Non-English speakers shouldn't have to navigate through the whole app before finding it
Eligibility checker was a small line of text at the bottom of the listing frame Made it a large, prominent button with a note saying "takes under 1 minute" Users were missing it entirely — it needed to feel like a core feature, not a footnote
Peer tips only appeared as a caption in the storyboard Built out as a full screen showing Maria Torres' name, year, major, quote, and three numbered tips Research showed peer voices are the most trusted information channel for first-gen students
Reminder confirmation cut off the reminder date Full confirmation screen with exact date, time, lock screen notification preview Users had no way of knowing if the reminder actually saved or when it would fire
GPA field asked for an exact number Changed to GPA ranges with a note that an estimate is fine and can be updated anytime Students who don't know their exact GPA shouldn't be stopped from checking eligibility
